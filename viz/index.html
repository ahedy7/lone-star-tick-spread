What you're seeing. Each hexagon is the effort-corrected share of iNaturalist tick sightings there that were lone star (lone star ÷ all hard-tick sightings), stabilized with empirical-Bayes shrinkage. Flip to RAW to watch single-sighting noise and effort bias drop out. Toggle CDC established footprint and Our leading edge to set our cumulative ever-detected counties against the official CDC footprint: leading-edge counties are candidates to watch (not confirmed expansion), and CDC-established counties we miss are observer-coverage blind spots (not absence of ticks). Caveat: the growing number of cells is partly a coverage artifact (more observers, more places sampled) — the honest signal is the contrast between surfaces and the slow northward push of the leading edge (~70 km over the decade), not raw area growth.
